How does our written-brief turn into a story script and ends up into a video production?

Each project varies, but generally, the production process can be categorized into three main phases:

1. Pre-production: This initial phase involves collaborating with you to understand your business thoroughly and collectively defining your video requirements. We may delve into questions such as your target audience, preferred publishing platforms, and key message objectives. Subsequently, we'll develop a video outline and/or detailed script, finalize budgets, and coordinate filming schedules with you.

2. Production: Also known as 'filming,' this stage encompasses capturing all necessary footage, such as interviews, event coverage, or product demonstrations. Filming may take place at your location, our studio, or other relevant sites aligned with the video's messaging.

3. Post-production: Following the footage capture, we commence the editing process, shaping the material into a cohesive narrative and incorporating graphics, titles, and music as needed. You'll have the opportunity to review one or more drafts, providing feedback before finalizing everything.

We offer cost-effective solutions without compromising on quality.

We work closely with clients to create custom packages that suit their financial constraints and project requirements.

How long does it take to make a video?

The duration required to complete a video project depends upon the project's scope of planning and execution. Often overlooked, the pre-production (planning) and post-production (editing) phases typically demand more time than the actual production (filming) phase.
The time it takes to produce your video can change based on how much work is needed in each phase. For example, a project that requires a lot of planning and coordination might take longer to plan. On the other hand, a project that relies heavily on editing after filming, with fewer live-action scenes, will require more time for editing.
So, the size of your project and how much you've already planned out will affect how long it takes to finish your video. This is why, at Shotspire, we prearrange the entire timeframe and tell you how much time the project will take after discussing your project.

What is the difference between marketing and advertising?

Advertising is a subset of marketing, focused specifically on promoting products or services through paid ads in various media channels. It involves creating persuasive content to reach and influence target audiences. On the other hand, marketing includes a broader range of activities that aim at identifying and satisfying customer needs. It includes market research, product development, pricing strategies, distribution channels, and customer relationship management, in addition to advertising.
